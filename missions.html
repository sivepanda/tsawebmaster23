Pre-Launch

Here at Retrograde we care deeply for the health and satisfaction of our customers. Due to that we have a mandatory health screening done by professionals to ensure against any negative effects that may occur in the conditions faced by astronauts. Some common health conditions that disqualify an individual from flight are: pregnancy, cardiovascular conditions, recent surgery or medical procedure and proneness to seizures. If you are disqualified from flight you will be offered a full refund of your ticket purchase.

If you have no disqualifying health conditions, your reservation will be set and a month before your launch date starts the training period. At Oxomo Space Center off the shores of Cancun, in Isla del Estrellas, you will be treated like royalty. As part of your experience, you get to spend the entirety of your training period in our state of the art facility.

Training

Due to the harsh environments faced by outer space travelers, passengers flying with Retrograde are required to undergo a month-long training process. The training starts with a week of basic online classes teaching you everything you need to know. The following in person week is full of many different hands-on lessons using our space vehicle mockups. The next week concludes the training process with an underwater buoyancy simulation of the experience in our large pool containing more space vehicle mockups for hands-on training. The final week consists of much review and a comprehensive exam to guarantee your safety in space. For repeat passengers the training process is not required as long as one can pass the in person exam.

Vehicles

Retrograde employs three different launch systems: Heimdall, Savitir, and Aether. Each launch system specializes in achieving different launch targets, and are specifically designed to be as safe and efficient as possible in reaching their respective targets.

Heimdall

The efficiency afforded by our ultra high efficiency Heimdall launch system is second to none, and allows us to ensure your flight is as cost effective and safe as possible. As our Low Earth Orbit launch system, Heimdall designed to lift a payload that solely holds the Thor orbiter and some additional cargo, maximizing space for safety systems and comfort on your orbit around Earth.

Savitir

Savitir is equally as efficient for its job. Taking you into Medium Earth Orbit into our state of the art space station, it boasts significantly more power and size to bring large amounts of cargo up into space. Savitir supports lifting equipment for laboratory experiments and movie studio equipment safely, while also providing luxurious comfort for all passengers on their way to our Fines Terrae space station that we operate in conjunction with a number of other travel and international space agencies.

Aether

Aether is the most powerful rocket currently in production. Taking full advantage of Retrograde’s groundbreaking research and harnessing of nuclear fusion to power our rockets, Aether is the sole rocket in production where all stages are nuclear powered. As a result, a trip to the moon that once took weeks now takes mere hours. Our Hyperion lander is the epitome of both comfort and cargo space, supporting a whopping 20 people and 30 tons of cargo into deep space. Aether is not only perfect for a vacation to the moon, but also for deep space cargo, supporting affordable missions to Mars and beyond.

Safety

We here at Retrograde value your safety. Using rockets to travel to space inherently carries a large amount of risk. In order to reduce this chance of serious injury we are Retrograde use state of the art engineering redundancies, techniques and failsafes. to make your trip as fun and safe as possible. One such example of redundancy is the extra set of parachutes integrated in our crew capsules.

Zero-Gravity Injuries

During spaceflight, astronauts are exposed to a microgravity environment where they experience a sensation of weightlessness. However, this lack of gravity can cause a host of physical problems, including muscle atrophy, bone density loss, and altered balance and coordination. As a result, the Retrograde training program will involve exercises to maintain strength, flexibility, and cardiovascular fitness, as well as simulations of zero-gravity conditions to prepare them for the real thing.

Decompression Sickness

During a spacewalk or other activities outside the spacecraft, astronauts are exposed to the vacuum of space. If not properly trained and prepared, this exposure can lead to decompression sickness, a potentially life-threatening condition caused by the rapid release of nitrogen bubbles in the bloodstream. Our program will help the astronauts receive extensive training on proper pressure and oxygen management to prevent decompression sickness.

Emergency Procedures

Astronauts must be prepared for a wide range of emergency scenarios, such as power outages, equipment failures, and medical emergencies. They undergo extensive training on emergency procedures and protocols, including how to troubleshoot and repair equipment, how to respond to medical emergencies, and how to evacuate the spacecraft in the event of a catastrophic failure.

Contamination

To prevent contamination of the spacecraft and the space environment, astronauts must undergo strict hygiene protocols and wear specialized clothing and equipment. Our specialized training is critical to prevent the spread of microbes and other biological materials that could harm both the crew and the spacecraft.

Motion Sickness

The sensation of weightlessness can also cause motion sickness, which can be debilitating for some astronauts. To mitigate the risk of motion sickness during training, astronauts may undergo simulations of spaceflight to help them acclimate to the experience and develop coping strategies.

Launch

Recovery

Thanks to recent advancements in rocket technology, we at Retrograde use fully reusable Vertical Takeoff, Vertical Landing (VTVL) rocket systems to cut down costs and improve recovery.

After your trip concludes the crew capsule will touchdown in the sea within 20 miles of our launch facility at Oxomo Space Center. After landing, our crew will be dispatched to pick you up and transport you to our facilities. After retrieval comes a quick medical check up to ensure no health conditions were caused or elevated by space. The trip concludes with a week of rest and recuperation at our 5 star hotel to familiarize yourself with living back on Earth.
