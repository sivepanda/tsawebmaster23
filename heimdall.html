Thor Lander

Thor is a lightweight and compact capsule that runs entirely on low-emission, ultra-reliable high-efficiency engines that allow us to achieve our negative net carbon emission goal by 2030. The three Hornet III-Superlite engines, using standard Methlyox fuel, pull a mind-boggling 300N of thrust, capable of 180 degree adjustment on two axes in seconds.

Second Stage

This is the first stage outside of the forces of Earth's atmosphere. With 2,000 kN (in a vacuum) of thrust on the single Dragonfly LPE (Light Propulsion Engine) powered by our state-of-the-art nuclear fusion reactor cell. This stage completes the crucial burns to ensure Low Earth Orbit (LEO) insertion is precise and clean.

First Stage

This is the very first stage, the most power occurs within this burn. Using a combination of 2 Dragonfly HLEs (HeavyLift Engine), each powered by the fusion of hydrogen atoms in the heart of the Dragonfly reactors, we achieve a jaw-dropping 20,000 kN at maximum thrust at sea level.
